Our Beneficiaries

Serving vulnerable and underserved communities with dignity, inclusion and equity.

Who GAIDI Serves

GAIDI prioritizes vulnerable and underserved populations that face barriers to health, education, livelihoods, participation, protection and social justice. Our beneficiary approach is guided by need, vulnerability, inclusion, fairness and community participation.

Primary Beneficiaries

These are the core groups directly targeted by GAIDI programs.

  • Persons with Disabilities
  • Vulnerable youth and adolescents
  • Women and girls at risk
  • Single mothers
  • Refugees and host communities
  • Orphans and vulnerable children
  • Economically disadvantaged households

Secondary Beneficiaries

These groups benefit through education, family and community support programs.

  • Primary school learners
  • Secondary school students
  • Tertiary institution students
  • Caregivers and families
  • Community-based groups

Institutional Beneficiaries

GAIDI also strengthens institutions that serve communities.

  • Schools and training institutions
  • Health facilities
  • Community organizations
  • Farmer groups and cooperatives
  • Disability organizations
  • Youth-led groups
  • Local government structures

Our Beneficiary Selection Principles

Need

Programs prioritize individuals and communities facing real development challenges.

Equity

Support is guided by fairness and responsible targeting.

Inclusion

Persons with disabilities and marginalized groups are meaningfully included.

Transparency

Selection processes are clear, fair and community-informed.

Our Inclusion Commitment

GAIDI believes that development is incomplete when vulnerable groups are left behind. We therefore integrate disability inclusion, gender equality, safeguarding, human rights, environmental responsibility and community participation across all programs and partnerships.

Support Vulnerable Communities

Partner with GAIDI to expand opportunities for persons with disabilities, youth, women, learners and underserved households.
