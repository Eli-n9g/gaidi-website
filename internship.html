Internship Opportunities

Gain practical experience while contributing to inclusive and sustainable community development.

Internship Program Overview

The GAIDI Internship Program provides students, graduates and early-career professionals with an opportunity to gain practical exposure in community development, public health, research, education, livelihoods, disability inclusion, communications and organizational administration.

Interns support real community-focused work while developing professional skills, leadership capacity, teamwork, documentation, innovation and evidence-based practice.

Available Internship Areas

Public Health

Health promotion, disease prevention, nutrition, WASH and community wellbeing.

Research & M&E

Data collection, monitoring, evaluation, documentation and evidence generation.

Disability Inclusion

Accessibility, sign language promotion, inclusion advocacy and disability empowerment.

Livelihoods

Agriculture, entrepreneurship, vocational skills and economic empowerment.

Education & Innovation

Mentorship, digital literacy, learning support and innovation initiatives.

Communications

Social media, photography, storytelling, website updates and public engagement.

Administration

Office support, reporting, coordination, records management and logistics.

Partnerships

Stakeholder mapping, proposal support, networking and partnership documentation.

Who Can Apply?

Students

University and tertiary students seeking field attachment, internship or practicum placement.

Recent Graduates

Graduates interested in gaining practical experience in development and community work.

Early-Career Professionals

Young professionals interested in nonprofit work, leadership and community engagement.

International Interns

Global learners interested in research, inclusion, development and community transformation.

Internship Expectations

Professionalism

Interns are expected to demonstrate discipline, integrity and respect.

Learning

Interns should be open to mentorship, feedback and practical field learning.

Safeguarding

Interns must protect children, vulnerable adults and community members from harm.

Reporting

Interns may support documentation, activity reports and evidence collection.
